Places to Visit

3 spots · historical roots & modern pulse

I've shortlisted 3 spots that offer a mix of Shanghai's historical roots and its modern pulse.

01

The Bund

外滩 Wàitān

Recommended

The Bund lies beside the Huangpu River. It blends historic Western architecture and modern skyscrapers across the river. It's a perfect place to feel the unique charm of old and new Shanghai. It offers a stunning contrast between the colonial-era architecture on one side and the futuristic Lujiazui skyline on the other. It's the best place to witness Shanghai's transformation over the last century.

Must-see Evening River views

02

Shanghai Museum

上海博物馆

Recommended

Shanghai Museum is widely considered one of China's best museums. It houses a world-class collection of ancient bronzes and ceramics. The collection also includes Ming and Qing furniture and ethnic crafts. It's very peaceful and full of history—perfect for taking your time and wandering around. If you're a culture or history buff, you'll love it.

Culture Indoor Free entry

Recommended time: Morning or afternoon (closes around 5:00 p.m.; last entry ~4:00 p.m.)

03

Yu Garden & Old Town

豫园 · 老城厢

Recommended

Yu Garden in Shanghai is a historic garden from the Ming Dynasty. With pavilions and buildings standing for more than 400 years, it's a favorite spot to soak up the old Shanghai vibe. You can wander around and shop here, and there are lots of unique Chinese-style stores to explore. The surrounding Old Town area is also great for trying local snacks and seeing traditional crafts, though it can be quite bustling.

History Old Shanghai Shopping

Food Experience

Shared meals · taste China together

Shanghai is a culinary melting pot. While it has its own unique local flavors, it's also the perfect place to experience the best dishes from all over China.

01

Hot Pot

火锅 · Huǒguō

Recommended

Hot pot has a history of some 3,000 years in China. To us, it means so much more than just tasty food. It's all about gathering together. Sitting around a steaming pot with friends instantly brings everyone closer. What's more, different regions have developed their own unique styles of hot pot over the years.

Must-try Social dining Evening

Sichuan Cuisine

川菜 · Chuāncài

Recommended

I noticed you mentioned Mapo Tofu on your personal website. This dish belongs to Sichuan cuisine — it originates from Sichuan Province in western China. Sichuan cuisine is hugely popular across the whole of China, featuring a spicy and numbing flavor profile. There are many delicious dishes in Sichuan cuisine that are well worth trying.

03

Benbang Cuisine

本帮菜 · Shanghai Local Dishes

Recommended

Benbang cuisine is Shanghai's local cooking style — slightly sweet, richly sauced, and deeply comforting. Try red-braised pork (hong shao rou), scallion oil noodles, or smoked fish. You can try the local specialties while you're in Shanghai.
